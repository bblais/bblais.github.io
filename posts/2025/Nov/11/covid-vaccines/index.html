Covid vaccines

Tue 11 November 2025 In #science

Articles:

  • https://www.lewrockwell.com/2023/02/joseph-mercola/autopsies-confirm-covid-jab-hazards-in-youth/
  • https://www.lewrockwell.com/2022/04/no_author/heart-damage-found-in-teens-months-after-second-pfizer-shot-study-shows/
  • https://www.lewrockwell.com/2024/10/no_author/another-study-finds-link-between-covid-shots-and-serious-heart-issues/

Our conversation started with your claims that heart conditions in youth were off the charts due to vaccines, that the mandates for medical professionals to be vaccinated was unfounded and unfair, and a general distrust of the effectiveness of the vaccines.   I pointed out that large studies have shown that the vaccines were very effective, especially compared to getting COVID yourself, even in younger populations.  I argue that, while there are always side effects to any medical treatment, and there are subpopulations that may require special attention that this does not detract from my original claim that the vaccines are some of the most effective treatments that we have and that their administration widely is well justified.

A very brief exploration confirms that, while vaccination can rarely trigger myocarditis (especially in adolescent males), the risks are way less for taking the vaccine than not.

  • The vaccine-induced risk is much lower than after COVID‑19: around 4–7 cases per 100,000 vaccinated teens, often mild, vs 130 per 100,000 children after COVID‑19 infection.

Also, several recent studies indicate that children who had received at least one COVID-19 vaccine dose before SARS‑CoV‑2 infection show significantly lower rates of post-infection heart issues than those who were unvaccinated.

Here are some -- these are super easy to find, by the way.

  • https://www.nature.com/articles/s41467-024-47745-z. 5.1 million children study
    • Unvaccinated children had higher risks of hospitalization due to seven adverse outcomes post-infection—including myocarditis and MIS-C.
    • In contrast, children vaccinated before infection did not show these elevated risks
  • https://www.ahajournals.org/doi/full/10.1161/CIRCULATIONAHA.122.059970. 43 million child study
    • Those infected after a vaccine dose had about half the myocarditis risk compared to those infected before being vaccinated
  • https://www.dal.ca/news/2024/04/01/covid-19-vaccination-cardiovascular-risks.html. 21 million people in the study
    • Vaccination notably reduced cardiovascular complications (like arrhythmias and blood clots) not only from acute COVID but also in the following year

In summary, you said that "heart conditions in youth were off the charts due to vaccines" and pointed to three articles that presented data on a very small number of cases (around 10 in one case, 20 in another, and the largest is around 950). In the latter case, they were looking at a very particular subset of people who had specific medical symptoms, and did not speak to the broader case of safety of vaccines. I pointed to three studies, in the multiple-millions of people, demonstrating that the risks for vaccines are far less than that of not getting vaccinated. This is how science is done. You can always fish out a specific case of someone who has a bad reaction to any medicine, whether a vaccine or motrin. The real question has to do with the overall effectiveness and safety across a population which can only be done with a large number. It is not at all convincing to point to this person or that to establish a point -- biology is messy and there will always be exceptions. But that's what they are -- exceptions not the rule.

Summaries of Three Articles from LewRockwell.com

1. Autopsies Confirm COVID‑Jab Hazards in Youth (Dr. Joseph Mercola, February 24, 2023)

Main Claims: - The article asserts that mRNA from COVID vaccines and the spike protein persist and circulate throughout the body longer than initially claimed by authorities. - It suggests evidence that free spike protein may be involved in cases of myocarditis following vaccination. - Autopsy findings in teens reportedly indicated heart damage consistent with stress-related cardiomyopathy (“Broken Heart Syndrome”), not classical myocarditis. - The author emphasizes these findings as suppressed truths about vaccine risks.

Source: LewRockwell.com oai_citation:0‡LewRockwell

Context & Limitations: - LewRockwell and Mercola.com are known for publishing anti-vaccine viewpoints and often lack backing from peer-reviewed science. - Broader evidence shows vaccine-associated myocarditis is rare, typically mild, and much less common than heart complications from COVID‑19 itself oai_citation:1‡TIME oai_citation:2‡People.com oai_citation:3‡BMJ.

2. Heart Damage Found in Teens Months After Second Pfizer Shot (Children’s Health Defense via Megan Redshaw, April 4, 2022)

Main Claims: - A peer-reviewed study in The Journal of Pediatrics found that over two-thirds of adolescents (16 evaluated via cardiac MRI) with vaccine-related myopericarditis had persistent heart abnormalities months later. - Persistent late gadolinium enhancement (LGE) was detected in 11 out of 16 patients, which can indicate heart injury or fibrosis—often associated with worse prognosis in traditional myocarditis.

Source: LewRockwell.com via Children’s Health Defense oai_citation:4‡LewRockwell

Context & Limitations: - The sample was small (16 teens), and findings are not definitive for the general population. - LGE indicates possible long-term effects but needs corroboration via larger, controlled studies. - Health agencies continue to characterize post-vaccine myocarditis as rare and generally mild, with benefits of vaccination far outweighing risks oai_citation:5‡BMJ oai_citation:6‡TIME.

3. Another Study Finds Link Between COVID Shots and Serious Heart Issues (Lifesite News via Calvin Freiburger, October 2, 2024)

Main Claims: - Reports on a peer-reviewed study published in Vaccine involving 949 heart attack patients in Madrid (March 2020 – March 2023). - Suggests that the combination of vaccination and prior COVID‑19 infection (“hybrid immunity”) was linked to severe heart failure and cardiogenic shock among patients with STEMI. - Hypothesizes that an exaggerated immune response could exacerbate heart attack severity, but acknowledges multiple limitations.

Source: LewRockwell.com via Lifesite News oai_citation:7‡LewRockwell

Context & Limitations: - The study is observational, with confounders like timing of infection/vaccination, patient heterogeneity, and lack of control over vaccine types or sequence. - Broader systematic reviews and real-world data do not show strong links between COVID vaccination and increased major cardiovascular events such as myocardial infarction, stroke, or arrhythmia oai_citation:8‡PMC oai_citation:9‡CIDRAP.

Final Takeaway

  • All three articles present provocative signals but rely on small-scale observations, opinion pieces, or limited evidence.
  • The broader, peer-reviewed scientific consensus supports that COVID-19 vaccines are safe and effective, with serious cardiovascular events being rare.
  • Risks from COVID-19 infection itself—including heart complications—are substantially greater than risks posed by vaccination oai_citation:10‡TIME oai_citation:11‡People.com oai_citation:12‡CIDRAP.
